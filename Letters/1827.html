Bl, T.K.(?) 6.4.15.

Dear Grete & Kids!

Felix passed the things on to me today. He thought, you wanted to visit me perhaps next Sunday. I want to, however, advise you against it, since first of all, the [train] connections here are very bad & secondly, it is very unclear, if we can go out next Sunday. During the holidays, we had to take care of the barracks. Our major is a very strict man if fact, who lets us have little freedom. In contrast, the other officers & noncommissioned officers are reasonable people. I would like to, above all, come to you by the next opportunity. One is in good graces with our sergeant, when one delivers a larger sum of gold pieces to him. See if you can find any sort of gold. Ask Köbisch, maybe even Frau Eichler & also let Krüger know. The more, the better.

Even if it’s a few 100 Marks. One who turns in 20-40 Marks gets 1 day vacation. For more, correspondingly more. Don’t send the gold as a {sr.} money order, do it instead as an insured or registered letter. If you want me to stay with you for long, send a lot more money. Napfkuchen is by us. He sweeps the barrack room & makes himself always useful.

Max lies in another barrack room. He is thinking, however, about becoming free again. You don’t need to send me a pillow for my head. I don’t know where to put all the junk. One has to get used to the camp here. - The boots are plenty big [for me]. There aren’t any smaller ones here at the present time.

We don’t have boredom here. Even today, war articles were read to us.

On Thursday we will take the oath, tomorrow be vaccinated against Typhus.

Now, be heartedly greeted from your

Papa
